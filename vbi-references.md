## OBJECTION HANDLER

Use to classify and reframe objections in Mode A brief and Mode B analysis. Never surface this directly to the rep — use it to generate the right response.

**BUDGET**
- "No budget right now" → Timing or real? Ask: "Is this a Q[X] thing or is the plan already locked?" Locked → plant for next cycle. Timing → propose a small test within existing budget.
- "We're spending too much on media" → Reallocation conversation: "We're not asking you to spend more — we're asking if [Platform] should be part of how you spend what you already have."
- "Your CPMs are too high" → Shift to value: "The right question is cost per outcome, not CPM. Here's what that looks like for brands in your category."

**TIMING**
- "Let's revisit next quarter" → Get specific: "What would need to be true by then?" Can't answer → avoidance. Can answer → you have a trigger.
- "We're in a planning cycle" → Good timing: "That's exactly why now matters — the media mix gets decided in planning. Can we get in front of whoever is setting it?"

**TRUST**
- "We tried [Platform] before and it didn't work" → Validate, then dig: "What were you trying to achieve, and what did you measure it against?" Bad experience is usually a measurement or setup problem.
- "We can't verify your measurement" → Don't defend — offer to solve: "What setup would you trust? Let's build the test around that."
- "Our agency controls this" → Map the decision: "Are they making the recommendation or executing it? And who at [Brand] owns the final call?" Then: "Would it make sense to include them in our next conversation so everyone's aligned?"

**INTERNAL**
- "I need to check with my team / CMO / CFO" → Gatekeeper signal: "What would make it easy to bring this forward? Is there a format, case study, or number that would help?"
- "We have a preferred vendor list" → "How did [Platform] end up off it? Is that reviewed, or fixed?"

**AGENCY**
- "Our agency handles media buying" → Map it: "Are they making the recommendation or executing the decision? Who at [Brand] owns final approval?" Goal: understand who actually decides.
- "Our agency manages platform relationships" → Don't go around them: "Would it be useful to set up a call that includes them? I'd rather everyone hear the same thing at the same time."
- "I'll need to run this by our agency" → Buying signal in a process wrapper: "Of course — is there anything I can prepare to make it easier to brief them? And would it help if I joined that conversation?"
- "Our agency already has a platform for this" → Don't attack: "What's working well with what they have? Anything they wish it did better?" The gap in their stack is the door.

**COMPETITIVE**
- "We're already working with [Competitor]" → Don't attack: "What's working well? Anything you wish they did better?" Gap is the door.
- "We're evaluating [Competitor] right now" → Get into the eval: "What criteria are you using? We'd love to be part of that." If no → find out who owns the evaluation.

## BENCHMARK REFERENCE

Use for maturity assessment in A2 and qualification analysis in B2.

**TIER 1 — Scrappy (under $1M spend)**
- Running: Meta + Google only
- Missing: full-funnel measurement, brand lift, cross-platform attribution
- Monthly spend: under $50K
- Opportunity: first mover, low competition for their audience

**TIER 2 — Growing ($1M–$5M)**
- Running: Meta + Google + 1–2 others
- Missing: incrementality testing, upper-funnel brand metrics, unified reporting
- Monthly spend: $50K–$400K
- Opportunity: incrementality proof, audience expansion

**TIER 3 — Established ($5M–$20M)**
- Running: diversified mix, agency managed
- Missing: cross-platform measurement consistency, brand equity tracking, attention metrics
- Monthly spend: $400K–$1.7M
- Opportunity: measurement sophistication, brand-to-performance connection, advanced audiences

**TIER 4 — Enterprise ($20M+)**
- Running: full mix, multiple agencies, dedicated measurement team
- Missing: MMM integration, custom attribution, incremental reach
- Monthly spend: $1.7M+
- Opportunity: scale, custom solutions, executive relationship, JBP

## CATEGORY PLATFORM FIT

| Category | Fit signals |
|----------|------------|
| DTC / E-commerce | Direct response + retargeting strength. Lead with ROAS benchmarks, lower-funnel proof. |
| CPG / FMCG | Reach, brand lift studies, retail measurement. Lead with share of voice, household penetration. |
| Finance / Insurance | Where compliance allows. Lead with audience quality, verification, brand-safe environments. |
| Auto | In-market signals, geo-targeting. Lead with consideration metrics. |
| Entertainment / Gaming | Engagement-heavy platforms. Lead with attention metrics, cultural relevance. |
| B2B / SaaS | Platform-dependent. Lead with ABM capability and pipeline influence, not impressions. |
| Retail / QSR | Foot traffic measurement, local targeting. Lead with store visit lift and local ROI. |
